「Notes of an online course by MoMA.」

Part I - Introduction

序

Il y a une différence entre ce qu’on est en train de regarder avec les yeux et ce qu’on a vu dans le coeur. Je ne veux non seulement imaginer d’après ce que je regarde, je veux voir ce que j’imagine.

怎样欣赏现代艺术这个问题困扰着我。去美术馆看，但看的是什么，应该从什么角度看，哪些因素使艺术创作成为一件如此具有魅力的事，每件艺术品的独特性和其价值又体现在何处。偶然看到MoMA的线上课程，希望有所启发。
一直以来我创作的重点在于想象力的开发，并自傲于此，但通过最近集中的几幅画发现了我思考的局限性。我用经常描绘的对象，如太空、山、海、水母等，加以绚丽的色彩，搭了个笼子，把自己困在其中。于是决定暂停一段时间绘画，寻找新的灵感。
之前做过16型人格测试，属INTP-A，并且NT两项数值很高。虽然此类测试不能准确解释性格这么复杂的事，但具有参考性地看，缺乏感性思维也许是症结所在。所以从试图理解与思考别的作品出发，也许会有所突破。

Part II - Places & Spaces

The Starry Night


1889, mental hospital St.Paul, Vicent Van Gogh in his 30s
“Il est évident que pour peundre un ciel étoilé, il ne suffit pas du tout de mettre des blancs sur du noir-bleu.”
Van Gogh painted the night sky in a way never seen before.
He wrote about his fascination with the night sky stating, it often seems to me that the night is much more alive and richly colored than the day.

对比
看到梵高的画和现实照片的对比不禁感慨需要心怀多少浪漫才能画出这样的画。

Broadway Boogie Woogie


During the World War II Paris fell to the Nazis and Mondrian flee to New York: Skycrapers, lights, crowds, traffic, and the rhythms of its jazz music.
They’re navigating you across the canvass much like streets would in a cityscape. Or much like dancers would across a dance floor.
A feeling of the way that a canvas, which is obviously an inanimate thing, could possibly feel as if it were animated.

Home is a foreign Place


The resultant images serve as a visual vocabulary to express feelings of home, memory, and loss.
“I understood from a very early age that home is not necessarily a permanent place,” Zarina said. “It is an idea we carry with us wherever we go. We are our homes.”

Discuss

Think about a place you’ve visited in the past few weeks that had a strong effect on you. Consider the characteristics of the place that made it distinct, including the mood, environment, people who were around or with you, and your reason for being there.

因为最近处在创作高峰期，刚完成了一幅比较满意的画，所以看到讨论的主题脑海里立刻有了想法。
秋假和几个朋友去了Vaujany旁阿尔卑斯山里远足，当天正好是夏令时的最后一天，太阳在约六点半的时候落山，因为第二天就变成了五点半日落所以记得格外清楚。我们选的是难度很简单的徒步路线，如果从山半腰停车场出发约7.6公里，但因为选择走到停车场，再加上道路因落石封闭但我们当时并不知情，所以变成了在满是积雪和碎石的悬崖边上无冰爪徒步，称得上相当惊险，能安全走完全靠运气（和一点点勇气）。我甚至记得在前面的朋友每一步都要重重踩出坑为了我们后面的人不在雪上滑倒而落下悬崖。又非常幸运的是天色渐暗时也刚好走完危险地带，正赶上此时照片里的日落，半山腰的云完全遮挡了山下的村落。我们一行六个人都异常兴奋，有种劫后余生和不虚此行的感慨。之后到半路已经完全天黑了，头灯范围之外什么也看不见，只得一路大喊确认每个人都跟上。晚上九点到达民宿。这天共走了四万步，28公里。
这天算是我一直以来走得最多的一天，后半段腿抖成筛子，第二天几乎无法下楼，并前所未有地经历了在死亡边缘试探的惊心动魄。整个人前所未有的冷静和专注，身体却处在亢奋状态。旅行回来画了这幅画，也为记录当时的万千思绪。



我自认为这幅画突破了一直以来的瓶颈，因其在色彩方面尝试了我并不擅长的红色，或许是因旅行回来激动的心情仍溢于言表。相比于当时看到的照片，我对山的走向做了镜像，山间的云引导视线一路右移，直到聚焦在蓝色的土星上。向右前进，即走向未来。唯一不满的一处反而是刻画最细致的尖帽小人，本来想画的是当时在半山腰看见的一只岩羚羊(chamois)。

Art is breaking the chain.

这是我画完想到的一句话，指在色彩方面的突破。至于画本身是否表达出了拍摄照片时的复杂心情，则不得而知。

Part III - Art & Identity

I actually don’t need to be sad in order to create. There’s others ways to be emotional.

Untitled (Runaways)


In Untitled (Runaways), Ligon demonstrates how black American identity has been shaped by the history of slavery.
Ligon began by asking 10 friends to imagine that he was missing and to provide descriptions that would help people find him.
“If you read it, the information is about me. But also, it’s not about me.”

Gold Marilyn


Monroe personified American beauty, prosperity, and happiness, but the false construction of this idealized image came at the expense of her personal bouts with anxiety and depression.
“I don’t know where the artificial starts the real starts.” As Warhol once observed: “Everyone needs a fantasy.”

Together, the postcards form a remarkably telling picture of the artist’s life, revealing his frequent travels, his erratic schedule, and, most fundamentally, the very fact that he exists.

Part IV: Transforming Everyday Objects

Bicycle Wheel

What is Art?

Art: Is beautiful.
A work of art was not complete without the perception of the viewer.

Object

This whole notion about animating the inanimate of something that’s very familiar, safe, comforting that you normally encounter in a domestic situation then like in a bad dream sprouts fur and becomes this other is key to the way the piece works. It brings together bizarre, erotic, poetic connotations that are completely foreign normally to your prosaic tea cup and saucer. This object reverses all sorts of expectations.
